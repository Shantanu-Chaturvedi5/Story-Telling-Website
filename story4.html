Chapter 1: The Rise of King Aldric

King Aldric the Lionheart was known as the greatest ruler of the Kingdom of Valdris. He was strong in battle, wise in council, and beloved by his people. Under his reign, the kingdom prospered, its borders secured by his conquests, and its people thriving under just laws. But as Aldric expanded his realm, he gained many enemies—both outside and within his court. Among his closest advisors was Lord Edric, a man Aldric had once saved in battle. Unknown to the king, Edric secretly harbored resentment, believing he deserved more power.

Chapter 2: The Whispered Lies and the Betrayal at Midnight

As Aldric prepared for his greatest campaign against the rival Empire of Dravenmoor, Edric saw his chance. Spreading whispers through the court, he sowed doubts about Aldric’s rule, convincing nobles that the king was growing too ambitious, that war would bring ruin, and that Aldric sought to make himself an emperor. Slowly, Edric built alliances with those who feared Aldric’s power, promising them wealth and titles once the king was overthrown.

On the eve of battle, Aldric celebrated with his men, confident in their strength. But that night, as the army slept, Edric and his co-conspirators struck. They opened the gates of the camp to the enemy and turned Aldric’s own guards against him. The king awoke to chaos—flames consuming his banners, the sounds of clashing steel, and the realization that his own men had betrayed him. As he fought through the treacherous ranks, his once-loyal friend, Edric, drove a blade into his side, whispering, “The kingdom needs a new ruler.”

Chapter 3: The Legend Lives On

Aldric, wounded and surrounded, fought with the fury of a lion. He cut down dozens before his strength began to wane. As dawn broke over the battlefield, he stood bloodied and alone, staring at the army that had once followed him. The new rulers of Valdris declared him a traitor, stripping him of his crown. With chains around his wrists, he was led away from his own kingdom, condemned to exile.

Though Aldric was gone, his legend endured. The people whispered of his betrayal, and many still held loyalty to the fallen king. In the distant mountains, where exiles and warriors gathered, Aldric did not fade into obscurity. He watched as Valdris fell into corruption under Edric’s rule. One day, when the kingdom was at its weakest, the Lionheart would return—not as a king, but as a conqueror seeking justice.

Some thrones are lost, but some kings never truly fall.
